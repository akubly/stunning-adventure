**Context:** Initial proposal carried `pray` as a first-class verb. Aaron's devil's-advocate process tested this against an agent-perspective acid test.

**Process:** Aaron questioned whether `pray` as a standalone verb (separate from `integrate`) could survive scrutiny. Cassima was asked to model what "prayer" semantically reduces to. Through iterative questioning, the team discovered that `pray` semantically collapsed into `integrate(kind=aspiration)` — prayers are aspirations encoded into the commitment system.

**Verdict (c):** 
- `pray` retired as a first-class verb
- `commit` introduced with full mechanics: hot tier, registry, retire, future `commit_floor`
- Aspirations now encoded as `kind=aspiration` within `integrate` verb
- Integration framing: a system property (not just initial encoding), grounded in Jungian conceptual model
